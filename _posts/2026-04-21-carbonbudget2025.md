Check out [Global Carbon Budget 2025]( https://essd.copernicus.org/preprints/essd-2025-659/). The McKinley group's LDEO-HPD observation-based product is included for estimates for the ocean carbon sink from 1959-2024. 

Below is an animation for the cumulative carbon budget for 1850-2024. 

![GCB 2025 cumulative budget, code by Marit Jentoft-Nilsen of NASA ]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/historical_carbon_budget_bar_chart_2025.mp4){:height="85%" width="85%"} 

There were substantial adjustments to the land use source, land sink and ocean sink estimates for GCB2025, presented in a separate paper [Friedlingstein et al. 2026](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-09802-5), with a invited comment by Professor McKinley [here](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-03981-x.epdf). These adjustments substantially impact our view of the cumulative sources and sinks of anthropogenic carbon since 1850, as seen here in this side-by-side comparison of 2023 from [last year's animation]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/historical_carbon_budget_bar_chart_2024.mp4) and this new one. 


![GCB 2024 vs GCB2025 in 2023 ]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/GCB2024vs2025_in2023_cumulative.png){:height="85%" width="85%"}
